About me

Currently, I am a LABEX-MILYON postdoctoral fellow in the MC2 team of LIP, ENS de Lyon, mentored by Édouard Bonnet. Previously, I was a C.V. Raman Postdoctoral Fellow at the Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru (IISc) mentored by Prof. L.S. Chandran. Before joining IISc, I was a Senior Research Fellow at Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology, Delhi (IIITD) under the supervision of Prof. Rajiv Raman and Prof. Syamantak Das. I completed my PhD from the department of Advanced Computing and Microelectronics Unit (ACMU), Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata (ISI-Kolkata) under the supervision of Prof. Sandip Das.

Research interests

My research interests lies in graph algorithms , graph theory and geometric algorithms. More specifically, I am interested in algorithmic aspects of metric graph theory, geometric intersection graph theory, and approximation algorithms for geometric optimisation problems. Some of my favourite algorithmic problems are metric-based covering problems (Geodetic sets, Isometric path cover, Induced path cover), complexity of recognizing geometric intersection graph classes, and domination problems on geometric intersection graphs. Recently, I have started working on graph clustering problems. Please see my Publications, Talks pages, for more details. Please consult my google scholar or DBLP to see the list of publications.
